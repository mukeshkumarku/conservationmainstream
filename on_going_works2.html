Pyaraneeshwarar Temple

Udayarpalayam, TamilNadu

Situated close to Gangaikonda Cholapuram which belongs to the Thanjavur group of temples is a tiny village that boasts the magnificent Shiva temple that was supposedly established during the Chola times and major additions happened during the Vijayanagara and Maratha rule over this area. The beautiful wall murals that align the walls of the Madapally (temple kitchen) belong to the Maratha era which is prominently identifiable due to the usage of bright cobalt dyes and stark burnt sienna.

The artistry is elaborate and detailed; in recent times suffer fungal growth and dampness. Adjacent to the temple complex is the Gandeepa Theertham. It is believed that Arjuna created this tank for drinking by digging the ground with his bow called Gandeepa. It is also a local belief that Ganesha helped him in his feat. Hence, the Ganesha of this temple is been called "Vil Valaitha Vinayagar"(Ganesha who bent the bow). Historically many religious institutions were functioning on the banks of Gandeepa Theertham, Today only two remain. The records of U.Ve. Swaminathaiyer's travels to Udayarpalayam help us understand the town as it were around a century ago.
